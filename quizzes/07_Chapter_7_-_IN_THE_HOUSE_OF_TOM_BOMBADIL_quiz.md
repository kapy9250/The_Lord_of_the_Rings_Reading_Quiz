# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 07_Chapter_7_-_IN_THE_HOUSE_OF_TOM_BOMBADIL.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Frodo’s internal reaction to meeting Goldberry—noting that the delight was "less keen and lofty" than elven-voices but "deeper and nearer to mortal heart"—suggests that Goldberry represents:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "He stood as he had at times stood enchanted by fair elven-voices; but the spell that was now laid upon him was different: less keen and lofty was the delight, but deeper and nearer to mortal heart; marvellous and yet not strange."

- **A.** A domestic ideal that Frodo finds easier to relate to than the "high" magic of the Elves.
- **B.** A primordial, earth-bound divinity that is intimately connected to the tangible, biological world of "mortal" experience.
- **C.** A sign that the hobbits' sensitivity to "lofty" magic is being eroded by their proximity to the Ring.
- **D.** A deceptive enchantment designed to soothe the hobbits into abandoning their quest.
- **E.** The personification of the Shire's "ideal" wife, representing a lost social innocence.

### Question 2 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**
