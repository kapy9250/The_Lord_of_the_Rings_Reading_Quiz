### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Pippin’s impression of Treebeard’s eyes primarily suggests that Treebeard represents:**

**Correct Answer: B. A consciousness that exists on a geologic or botanical timescale, where the "individual" is subsumed by a vast, accumulated experience of the natural world.**

**Why this is correct:**
Pippin describes the "enormous well" of memory and "long, slow, steady thinking." Treebeard's eyes suggest a perspective that has considered "inside affairs for endless years." He is a being whose very sense of time and identity is rooted in the forest, making him a personification of nature's ancient awareness.

- **A** is a "Moralistic Misreading" that suspects a "predatory" nature.
- **C** is an "Academic Over-Interpretation" using the term "dream-state."
- **D** is a "Plausible Misreading" about "waked up kings."
- **E** is an "Over-Interpretation" about "inability to distinguish thoughts."

### Question 2 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**Treebeard’s refusal to tell his "real name" illustrates a belief that:**

**Correct Answer: B. Identity is a dynamic, unfolding history where language and existence are inextricably linked, making a "real name" a total summation of a being's life.**

**Why this is correct:**
Treebeard says "real names tell you the story of the things they belong to." In Entish, a name is a cumulative record. For someone as old as Treebeard, a name encompasses the "story" of his life. This reflects a philosophical depth where language and existence are linked.

### Question 3 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Treebeard’s description of Saruman’s face characterizes the wizard’s descent into evil as:**

**Correct Answer: B. A progressive loss of transparency and reciprocal empathy, where Saruman has "shuttered" his internal world to conceal a cold, industrial agenda.**

Treebeard notes Saruman’s face became like a wall with "shutters inside." This metaphor describes Saruman's isolation and his shift from a seeker of wisdom to a "Power" with a "mind of metal and wheels." The "shutters" represent the lack of reciprocity.

### Question 4 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**What does Treebeard’s suspicion that Saruman has "blended the races" suggest about his moral boundaries?**

Treebeard is horrified by the idea of Orc-Man hybrids, calling it a "black evil." As a "tree-herd" who values the "lore of Living Creatures," he perceives the deliberate corruption of biological identity as the ultimate crime against creation.

### Question 6 `[ACN/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**The story of the loss of the Entwives serves as a metaphor for:**

**Correct Answer: B. The fundamental tension between "the wild" and "the cultivated," illustrating a tragic divergence in the perception of beauty that leads to a biological and social rift.**

The Ents loved "wild woods," while the Entwives desired "order" in gardens. This is a classic theme: the conflict between wilderness and agriculture. The "loss" is the mythic result of this incompatibility, resulting in a species that is "fading."
