### Question 1 `[ACN/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**The description of the Brown Lands serves to:**

**Correct Answer: B. Illustrate the total, life-extinguishing impact of the Enemy’s historical presence, where the land itself has been "blasted" into a state of permanent ontological desolation.**

**Why this is correct:**
The text describes an "unfriendly waste" without green or stone. This emptiness represents the absolute negation of life that Sauron brings. Unlike the living "Old Forest," these lands are "withered" and "formless," a manifestation of the Enemy's scorched-earth history.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" about a "recent campaign."
- **C** is a "Plausible Misreading" that suspects "sensory fatigue."
- **D** is an "Over-Interpretation" contrasting it with "unnatural fertility" in Lorien.
- **E** is a "Half-Truth" about "tactical reasons" that misses the *thematic* weight.

### Question 2 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Sam’s changing attitude toward trees reflects:**

**Correct Answer: B. The psychological shift from a fear of "lurking" internal malice to a greater fear of the "naked" vulnerability exposed by open terrain in a war zone.**

**Why this is correct:**
In the Old Forest, Sam feared what was *inside*. On the River, he realizes the open plains offer no shelter. His desire for trees is a realization that concealment is now more valuable than absolute openness in a "shelterless land."

- **C** is an "Over-Interpretation" about his "lack of imagination."
- **D** is a "Comedy" distractor about "boats warping."
- **E** is a "Lore-Trap" about "mallorn-trees as friends."
