# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 26_Chapter_4_-_TREEBEARD.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "One felt as if there was an enormous well behind them, filled up with ages of memory and long, slow, steady thinking... it felt as if something that grew in the ground... had suddenly waked up, and was considering you with the same slow care that it had given to its own inside affairs for endless years."

- **A.** A dangerous predator who is sizing up his prey.
- **B.** A consciousness that exists on a geologic or botanical timescale, where individual moments are subsumed by a vast, accumulated experience of the natural world.
- **C.** A senile old man who has forgotten his own name.
- **D.** A sign that the forest is actually a magical illusion.
- **E.** Treebeard’s desire to become a philosopher.
