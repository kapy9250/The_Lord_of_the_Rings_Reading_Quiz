# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 32_Chapter_10_-_THE_VOICE_OF_SARUMAN.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "Suddenly another voice spoke, low and melodious... desire awoke in them by swift agreement to seem wise themselves. ... all that it said seemed wise and reasonable... But none were unmoved; none rejected its pleas and its commands without an effort of mind and will..."

- **A.** A literal magical spell that overrides the listener's physical body.
- **B.** A sophisticated form of psychological and rhetorical manipulation that exploits the listener’s own desire for intellectual and moral validation, creating a false sense of shared wisdom.
- **C.** Evidence that Saruman is actually the most reasonable person in the world.
- **D.** A sign that the listeners are all very unintelligent.
- **E.** A way to make people forget their own names.
