### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Pippin’s self-deprecating thought primarily reflects:**

**Correct Answer: B. A deep-seated existential crisis where the trauma of captivity strips him of his sense of individual agency, reducing his self-worth to a purely utilitarian status.**

**Why this is correct:**
Pippin explicitly asks "What good have I been?" and calls himself a "nuisance" and "luggage." His capture has stripped him of agency. By comparing himself to "luggage," he expresses the trauma of being an object moved by others' wills, illustrating the psychological impact of his perceived uselessness compared to the heroes he follows.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" about "physical weight."
- **C** is a "Plausible Misreading" that suspects a "tactical attempt" to be ignored.
- **D** is a "Lore-Trap" that treats genuine distress as a "humorous coping mechanism."
- **E** is an "Academic Over-Interpretation" about "moral failure."

### Question 2 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**The conflict between Uglúk and Grishnákh reveals a fundamental tension between:**

**Correct Answer: B. The competing, autonomous political agendas of Isengard and Mordor, where the coalition of evil is fragmented by the rivalries of its dark masters.**

**Why this is correct:**
Grishnákh represents Mordor and the "Great Eye," while Uglúk is a "servant of Saruman" and the "White Hand." Their quarrel is not just personal; it is institutional. They serve different masters who are already competing for the Ring. This shows that the forces of evil are not a monolith, but a fragile coalition of competing tyrannies.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Plausible Misreading" about "Orc-religions."
- **C** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" about "logistics."
- **D** is a "Half-Truth"; while Saruman *is* betraying Sauron, the tension in the scene is about the *Orcs'* conflicting loyalties.
- **E** is an "Academic Over-Interpretation" using the term "social construction."

### Question 3 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Pippin’s use of the "gollum" noise illustrates:**

**Correct Answer: B. A sophisticated and high-stakes psychological gambit, where Pippin exploits Grishnákh’s suspected greed to manipulate him into a potentially advantageous betrayal.**

**Why this is correct:**
Pippin "catches" Grishnákh's thought and realizes the Orc is looking for the Ring. By imitating Gollum, he provides a "hook" to believe Pippin *has* it. This manipulation leads Grishnákh to abduct the hobbits, inadvertently taking them outside the Orcish camp and providing the opportunity for their eventual escape.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is an "Over-Interpretation" about "empathy with Gollum."
- **C** is a "Lore-Trap" linking the noise to "Orc-liquor."
- **D** is a "Comedy" distractor about "long-lost relatives."
- **E** is a "Lore-Trap" about "pre-programmed messages."

### Question 4 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**Uglúk’s healing of Merry’s wound is characterized as:**

**Correct Answer: B. A purely utilitarian and brutal restoration of a "commodity," where efficiency is prioritized over comfort, leaving a permanent physical mark of the Enemy’s "care."**

**Why this is correct:**
Uglúk's goal is not healing for well-being, but ensuring "usefulness for the Master." The result is a "brown scar" that Merry bears "to the end of his days," a physical symbol of the harsh and dehumanizing nature of Orcish "care." The Orcs "hoot" at his struggle, highlighting their lack of compassion.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Moralistic Misreading" that suspects "compassion."
- **C** is a "Plausible Misreading" about "superior industrial medicine."
- **D** is an "Over-Interpretation" about "recruiting Merry."
- **E** is a "Lore-Trap" about "binding souls to boxes."

### Question 7 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

### Question 10 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**The "sudden singing of many men" primarily signifies:**

**Correct Answer: B. The arrival of a redemptive, solar-aligned power that forcefully displaces the "nightmare" of the Orcs, restoring the earth to the light of day.**

**Why this is correct:**
The Rohirrim charge with the rising sun. Their "singing" and "great cry" provide a sharp, heroic contrast to the "hissing" and "snarling" of the Orcs. This represents the triumph of the heroic human spirit over the light-hating malice of Mordor.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Moralistic Misreading" calling it "noisy and undisciplined."
- **C** is a "Plausible Misreading" about "celebrating before victory."
- **D** is a "Plausible Misreading" about "musical performance vs efficiency."
- **E** is an "Over-Interpretation" about "Orcs being afraid of song."
