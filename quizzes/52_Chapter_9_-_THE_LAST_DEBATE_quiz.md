# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 52_Chapter_9_-_THE_LAST_DEBATE.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Legolas’s and Gimli’s conversation as they enter the City—where the Dwarf sees "good stone-work" and the Elf sees "dead houses" that need gardens—serves to:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'There is some good stone-work here,' he [Gimli] said... 'They need more gardens,' said Legolas. 'The houses are dead... the people of the Wood shall bring him birds that sing...'"

- **A.** Highlight their mutual lack of appreciation for human architecture.
- **B.** Characterize the City of Gondor as a site of potential rebirth, where the specialized "loves" of the different races (stone and growth) will be combined to restore the beauty of the returned King’s realm.
- **C.** Evidence that Legolas and Gimli are starting to argue again after the battle.
- **D.** A sign that the City is actually in ruins beyond all repair.
- **E.** A humorous commentary on the different tastes of Elves and Dwarves.

### Question 2 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Legolas’s reaction to the gulls—feeling "no peace shall I have again under beech or under elm"—indicates:

> **Relevant Passage:**

- **A.** His literal fear of being attacked by sea-birds.
- **B.** The awakening of the "sea-longing"—an inescapable, spiritual "homesickness" for the West that marks the beginning of his eventual departure from Middle-earth and his detachment from the lands he previously loved.
- **C.** Evidence that Legolas is becoming tired of the war.
- **D.** A sign that the gulls are actually spies for Sauron.
- **E.** His desire to find a new forest by the ocean.

### Question 3 `[CIM/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

- **A.** Gimli’s cowardice and his failure as a warrior.
- **B.** The absolute, near-supernatural potency of the King’s authority, which provided the spiritual "anchor" for his companions in a realm of "Unseen" terror that bypassed their natural physical hardiness.

### Question 4 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

- **D.** A sign that Aragorn is becoming as "dark" as the Enemy.

### Question 5 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

Legolas’s thought that Aragorn might have become a "great and terrible Lord... had he taken the Ring" reinforces:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'...I looked on Aragorn and thought how great and terrible a Lord he might have become... had he taken the Ring to himself. Not for naught does Mordor fear him. But nobler is his spirit...'"

- **C.** A sign that Aragorn is actually more powerful than Gandalf.

- **E.** His belief that Aragorn should have used the Ring to defeat the Dead.

### Question 9 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`
