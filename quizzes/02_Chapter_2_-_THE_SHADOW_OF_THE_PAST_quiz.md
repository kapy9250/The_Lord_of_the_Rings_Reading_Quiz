# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 02_Chapter_2_-_THE_SHADOW_OF_THE_PAST.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

### Question 1 `[SCA/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "The second disappearance of Mr. Bilbo Baggins was discussed... for a year and a day, and was remembered much longer than that. It became a fireside-story for young hobbits; and eventually Mad Baggins... became a favourite character of legend and lived on long after all the true events were forgotten."

- **A.** The hobbits possess a collective trauma that prevents them from discussing the unsettling specifics of the departure.
- **B.** The Shire community tends to domesticate and simplify disruptive events into safe, fictionalized narratives.
- **C.** The legend of "Mad Baggins" is a deliberate piece of propaganda used by elders to discourage younger hobbits from traveling.
- **D.** The Shire's oral tradition is more interested in moral instruction than in the preservation of factual history.
- **E.** The canonization of Bilbo into legend reflects a secret, repressed longing for adventure within the broader hobbit population.
