# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 61_Chapter_8_-_THE_SCOURING_OF_THE_SHIRE.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

### Question 1 `[SCA/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

The "new houses" at the Brandywine Bridge—described as "gloomy and un-Shirelike" with "narrow straight-sided windows"—symbolize:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "on the further side of the river they could see that some new houses had been built: two-storeyed with narrow straight-sided windows... all very gloomy and un-Shirelike."

- **A.** A successful modernization project that brings the Shire into alignment with Gondorian urban standards.
- **B.** The architectural and psychological "Mordorization" of the Shire, where organic domesticity is replaced by a sterile, utilitarian, and authoritarian aesthetic of control.
- **C.** Evidence that the hobbits have lost the "lore" of hole-building and are forced to imitate the Men of Bree.
- **D.** A sign that the population of the Shire has grown so rapidly that "quick and cheap" housing is now a necessity.
- **E.** A humorous commentary on the hobbits' inability to build straight walls without wizardly help.
