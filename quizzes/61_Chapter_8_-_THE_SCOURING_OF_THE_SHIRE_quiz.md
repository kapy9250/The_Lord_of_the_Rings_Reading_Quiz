# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 61_Chapter_8_-_THE_SCOURING_OF_THE_SHIRE.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[SCA/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "on the further side of the river they could see that some new houses had been built: two-storeyed with narrow straight-sided windows... all very gloomy and un-Shirelike."

- **A.** A successful and modern urban development project.
- **B.** The architectural and psychological "Mordorization" of the Shire, where the organic and hospitable traditions of hobbit-holes are replaced by a sterile, utilitarian, and authoritarian aesthetic.
- **C.** Evidence that the hobbits are becoming better at masonry.
- **D.** A sign that the population of the Shire is growing rapidly.
- **E.** A humorous comparison to the tall houses of Bree.
