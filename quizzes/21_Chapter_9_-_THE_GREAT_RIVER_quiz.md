# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 21_Chapter_9_-_THE_GREAT_RIVER.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[ACN/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

The description of the Brown Lands as a "formless slopes... as if fire had passed over them" primarily serves to:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "brown and withered they looked, as if fire had passed over them, leaving no living blade of green: an unfriendly waste without even a broken tree or a bold stone to relieve the emptiness."

- **A.** Describe a recent volcanic eruption in the area.
- **B.** Illustrate the total, life-extinguishing impact of the Enemy’s historical presence, where the land itself has been spiritually and physically "blasted" into a state of permanent desolation.
- **C.** Suggest that the hobbits are suffering from heatstroke.
- **D.** Explain why there are no Elves living in that region.
- **E.** Provide a tactical reason for why the Company should stay in their boats.

### Question 2 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Sam’s changing attitude toward trees—initially wishing them gone and then "wishing that the trees were still there"—reflects:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "The trees had seemed hostile before... now he wished that the trees were still there. He felt that the Company was too naked, afloat in little open boats in the midst of shelterless lands..."

- **A.** Sam’s inherent indecisiveness and lack of a stable perspective.
- **B.** The psychological shift from a fear of "lurking" internal dangers to a greater fear of the "naked" vulnerability exposed by open, defenseless terrain in a war zone.
- **C.** His belief that the Orcs are better at climbing trees than the hobbits are.
- **D.** A sign that he is starting to miss the gardening work of the Shire.
- **E.** His realization that the mallorn-trees were actually his friends.

### Question 3 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

- **C.** Boromir’s admiration for Frodo’s courage.
- **D.** A sign that Boromir is planning a surprise party for the hobbits.
- **E.** Boromir’s confusion about why Frodo is sitting in the leading boat.

### Question 5 `[CIM/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

### Question 6 `[ACN/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**

### Question 8 `[CIM/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

Boromir’s argument to abandon the boats and strike westward into Rohan suggests that his primary motivation is:

> **Relevant Passage:**

### Question 10 `[CIM/SCA]` `[Advanced]`
