# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 58_Chapter_5_-_THE_STEWARD_AND_THE_KING.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Éowyn’s statement—"It needs but one foe to breed a war, not two... and those who have not swords can still die upon them"—reflects a worldview of:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'It needs but one foe to breed a war, not two, Master Warden,' answered Eowyn. 'And those who have not swords can still die upon them. ... it is not always good to be healed in body.'"

- **A.** A desire for absolute world peace.
- **B.** A "heroic realism" that rejects the passive safety of the non-combatant, asserting that in a total war, the refusal to fight is a form of spiritual and physical vulnerability that does not prevent death but merely dishonors it.
- **C.** Evidence that Éowyn is becoming as bloodthirsty as an Orc.
- **D.** Her belief that the Warden is actually a secret enemy.
- **E.** A sign that she has lost her faith in the power of healing.
