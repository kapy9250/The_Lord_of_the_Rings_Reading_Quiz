### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Éowyn’s statement reflects:**

**Correct Answer: B. A "heroic realism" that rejects passive safety, asserting that in total war, the refusal to fight does not prevent death but merely dishonors it.**

**Why this is correct:**
Éowyn argues war is a condition imposed by an aggressor. Her rejection of gathered herbs while the Dark Lord gathers armies reflects her belief that life is only worth living if one has the agency to defend it.

---

### Question 2 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Faramir’s decision distinguishes his authority as:**

**Correct Answer: B. A disciplined stewardship that respects specialized expertise and recognizes strength lies in the patient endurance of "dull tasks."**

**Why this is correct:**
Faramir’s humility is his "High" quality. He refuses to use his rank to override medical authority. He identifies her restlessness as a form of waywardness compared to the firmness needed for dull tasks.

---

### Question 3 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**The "softening" of Éowyn symbolizes:**

**Correct Answer: B. The spiritual "thaw" where Faramir’s empathetic love begins to dissolve the defensive barrier she built against rejection.**

**Why this is correct:**
Éowyn’s "frost" was a result of unreturned love for Aragorn. Faramir’s "grave tenderness" provide the first real emotional relief she has felt. The "yielding" marks her journey away from seeking death.

---

### Question 4 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**Faramir’s gift serves to:**

**Correct Answer: B. Symbolically "re-clothe" her identity, identifying her as a high-noble inheritance while preparing her for her role in Gondor.**

**Why this is correct:**
By wrapping her in his mother's mantle, Faramir is physically manifesting his love and recognition of her "High" nature. It places the "White Lady of Rohan" within the historical context of Gondor’s nobility.

---

### Question 5 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Faramir’s dream suggests:**

**Correct Answer: B. A deep, ancestral memory of the fall of Numenor, providing a mythic framework to understand the Shadow as a recurring catastrophe.**

**Why this is correct:**
Faramir identifies the mountain of darkness as something that "reminds me of Numenor." This ancestral perspective allows him to see the "Doom" as part of a larger cosmic cycle of cataclysm.

---

### Question 6 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Éowyn’s final resolution marks her:**

**Correct Answer: B. A profound spiritual Renovation, rejecting the sterile pursuit of "renown" through death in favor of life-affirming preservation.**

**Why this is correct:**
She recognizes her previous desire for "glory" was a form of "seeking death." By choosing to be a healer, she aligns herself with the "White" power of Gondor (Aragorn’s hands).

### Question 7 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**Aragorn’s refusal signifies:**

**Correct Answer: B. The re-establishment of balanced order, where the King’s return validates and secures the legitimate "Trust" of the Stewardship.**

**Why this is correct:**
By giving the rod back, Aragorn acknowledges Faramir’s faithful service. He establishes a relationship of "High" partnership. The restoration is a completion of Gondor's law.

---

### Question 8 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Aragorn’s request emphasizes:**

**Correct Answer: B. A ritualistic acknowledgment of the "unseen" foundations of his kingship, identifying the sacrifice of the small and Wise as the true sources.**

**Why this is correct:**
Aragorn refuses to take the credit for his restoration. He identifies the "unimportant" hobbit and the "wizard" as the true "movers," establishing his kingdom on humility.

---

### Question 9 `[CIM/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**The discovery of the sapling symbolizes:**

**Correct Answer: B. The miraculous restoration of spiritual life, matching the King’s return with a sign of continuity from the beginning.**

**Why this is correct:**
The blooming is final proof of the "New Age." It confirms the "withered" state is over. The tree was "hidden," mirroring how the race of Elendil was hidden.

---

### Question 10 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Frodo’s feeling upon seeing Arwen suggests:**

**Correct Answer: B. The total "healing" of the world’s cycle, where the beauty of the Evenstar redeems the darkness from its association with the Enemy.**

**Why this is correct:**
Arwen represents beauty that shines *in* the night. Her arrival signifies that darkness is no longer poisoned. It marks the restoration of the "Evening" as a time of peace.
