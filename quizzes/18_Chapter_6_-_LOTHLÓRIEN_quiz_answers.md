### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Aragorn’s statement that "We must do without hope" primarily conveys:**

**Correct Answer: B. A transition from a motivation based on expected victory to a motivation based on grim duty and the refusal to be paralyzed by grief.**

**Why this is correct:**
Aragorn acknowledges the apparent hopelessness without Gandalf. However, he immediately counters this by calling the Company to action ("gird ourselves and weep no more"). This reflects a high heroic ethic: one does not perform a task because one expects to win, but because the task is right and necessary.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" that mistakes a heroic shift for "nihilistic conclusion."
- **C** is an "Over-Interpretation" that suspects a "tactical maneuver" rather than a genuine moral choice.
- **D** is a "Plausible Misreading" that frames the loss as a deliberate "test."
- **E** is a "Lore-Trap" that uses his name "Estel" (Hope) to create an ironic meaning not supported by the somber tone.

### Question 2 `[ACN/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**The description of the Mirrormere symbolizes:**

**Correct Answer: B. A sacred, preserved space where the "Seen" world of the present and the "Unseen" world of mythic antiquity coexist in a moment of visionary clarity.**

**Why this is correct:**
The Mirrormere is a place where stars appear in the reflection during daylight. This indicates a suspension of normal physical laws in favor of a deeper, spiritual truth. It connects the characters to the "unfallen" past of Durin’s Day.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" about "depth" and "clarity."
- **C** is a "Plausible Misreading" about "visual illusions."
- **D** is a "Lore-Trap" linking it to the "wraith-world."
- **E** is a "Half-Truth" about Orcs that misses the *miraculous* nature of the stars.

### Question 3 `[CIM/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**Aragorn’s gasp of wonder and his laugh upon seeing Frodo’s mithril-coat reveal:**

**Correct Answer: B. A moment of sudden, joyful relief and professional appreciation for the "providential" protection that saved the Ring-bearer.**

**Why this is correct:**
Aragorn was full of concern. Seeing the mithril explains the miracle. His laughter is an expression of delight at Bilbo's wisdom and the "kingly" protection that saved the quest. It marks a shift from his "heavy-hearted" state.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Moralistic Misreading" that suspects "amusement at the absurdity."
- **C** is a "Plausible Misreading" about "Baggins wealth."
- **D** is an "Over-Interpretation" about "possessive impulses."
- **E** is a "Lore-Trap" about Bilbo "keeping secrets from Gandalf."

Boromir's fear is rooted in Gondor's legends of Elven "sorcery." Aragorn suggests the "scathe" one receives is actually a form of "change." Boromir represents the "mortal" fear of being judged or transformed by a power that is fundamentally good but overwhelmingly high.

- **D** is a "Half-Truth" about "cultural shame."
- **E** is a "Moralistic Misreading" about any magic being "allied with the Shadow."
