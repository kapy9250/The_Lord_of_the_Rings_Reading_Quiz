# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 53_Chapter_10_-_THE_BLACK_GATE_OPENS.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Aragorn’s advice to the despondent Merry ("do not grudge [Pippin] his chance of peril") reveals his view of heroic participation as:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'...do not grudge him his chance of peril, for though he has done as well as his fortune allowed him, he has yet to match your deed. But in truth all now are in like danger.'"

- **A.** A competition for glory where each hobbit must try to outdo the other.
- **B.** A sacred and individual opportunity for "earning honour" that is uniquely tailored to each person’s fortune, where the "peril" is not a burden to be avoided but a necessary stage for moral and spiritual validation.
- **C.** Evidence that Aragorn prefers Pippin’s company over Merry’s.
- **D.** A sign that none of the hobbits are actually expected to survive.
- **E.** A plan to use Pippin as a political representative of the Shire.
