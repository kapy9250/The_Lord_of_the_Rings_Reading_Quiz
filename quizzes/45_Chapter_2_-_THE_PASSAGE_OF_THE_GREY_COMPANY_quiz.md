# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 45_Chapter_2_-_THE_PASSAGE_OF_THE_GREY_COMPANY.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Merry’s feeling that he is "unneeded baggage" and his subsequent decision to offer his sword to Théoden reveals:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "Merry felt more like unneeded baggage than ever... 'No good!' he thought. He drew his sword... 'May I lay the sword of Meriadoc of the Shire on your lap, Theoden King? Receive my service, if you will!'"

- **A.** A tactical desire to gain access to the King’s private war-council.
- **B.** A profound psychological need to transition from a passive, protected "child" role to an active, recognized participant in the heroic narrative.
- **C.** Evidence that the "Shire-values" of comfort are being replaced by a "Northern" blood-lust.
- **D.** His belief that the King is the only person in the muster who is "un-important" enough to notice him.
- **E.** A sign that Merry has been "enchanted" by the music of the Rohirrim.
