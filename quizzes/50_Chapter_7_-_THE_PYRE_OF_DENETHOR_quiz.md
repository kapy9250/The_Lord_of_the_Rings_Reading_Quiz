# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 50_Chapter_7_-_THE_PYRE_OF_DENETHOR.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Gandalf’s choice to return to the Citadel to save Faramir, despite hearing the "gathering sound of battle" at the Gate, illustrates a leadership priority of:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'I must go,' he said. 'The Black Rider is abroad... I have no time.' ... 'Maybe I can [save Faramir],' said Gandalf; 'but if I do, then others will die, I fear. Well, I must come...'"

- **A.** A tactical preference for protecting high-ranking officers over common soldiers.
- **B.** A "Higher" moral economy where the prevention of a spiritual and familial atrocity (a father murdering his son) takes precedence over immediate military utility, even at a calculated cost of lives on the field.
- **C.** Evidence that Gandalf is afraid of facing the Black Rider directly.
- **D.** His belief that the Rohirrim will win the battle without his help.
- **E.** A plan to use Faramir as a bargaining chip with Sauron.
