### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Merry’s thought characterizes his state as:**

**Correct Answer: B. The lingering residue of the Black Breath, where the physical effort of the climb is filtered through an internal landscape of despair.**

**Why this is correct:**
Merry is "weary" and "full of pain." He describes the ascent as a "meaningless journey in a hateful dream." His thought about the "tomb" reflects the spiritual "shadow" cast by the Witch-king.

---

### Question 2 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**Ioreth’s mention of the old lore serves to:**

**Correct Answer: B. Introduce the "High" diagnostic criterion for true kingship, framing Aragorn’s return as a restorative act of biological and spiritual renewal.**

**Why this is correct:**
Aragorn’s ability to heal the "Black Shadow" is a power that the Stewards lack. It shifts the definition of "King" from a mere wielder of a sword to a restorer of life.

---

### Question 3 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Aragorn’s decision to stay outside the City is motivated by:**

**Correct Answer: B. A high-stakes diplomatic caution, prioritizing the internal unity of the defense and the legal legitimacy of his claim over his own personal "glory."**

**Why this is correct:**
Aragorn explicitly states he fears "doubt and debate may arise." He knows that entering as a King while the City is in trauma could be seen as an act of opportunistic "supplanting."

### Question 4 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Aragorn’s analysis characterizes the wound as:**

**Correct Answer: B. A complex, psychosomatic and spiritual collapse, where the physical wound acted as a gateway for a deeper "Shadow" fostered by emotional trauma.**

**Why this is correct:**
Aragorn notes that the "hurt" was not "deep or vital." The real danger is the "Black Breath" and the "grief." Faramir's "staunch will" was eroded by the "Shadow" long before he was hit.

---

### Question 5 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**The Herb-master’s dismissal illustrates a decline in:**

**Correct Answer: B. The loss of "High" lore and its replacement by a sterile, academic intellectualism that can no longer perceive spiritual potency.**

**Why this is correct:**
The Herb-master is "learned in tongues" but lacks "wisdom." He treats the rhyme as "doggerel" because he cannot measure its effect.

---

### Question 6 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**The "living freshness" and memory of "dewy mornings" represent:**

**Correct Answer: B. The active, spiritual "exorcism" of the Black Shadow through the release of a primordial, unfallen vitality that exists in the "king's hand."**

**Why this is correct:**
The fragrance is described as "like a memory... of unshadowed sun." This is more than just a good smell; it is an atmospheric shift. It "lightens" the hearts of all who stand by.

---

### Question 7 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Faramir’s first words signify:**

**Correct Answer: B. A moment of total, intuitive recognition and unreserved allegiance, where the "Steward" immediately perceives and accepts the returned royal authority.**

**Why this is correct:**
Faramir wakes with a "light of knowledge and love." He does not ask who Aragorn is; he simply calls him "lord" and "king." This provides the moral and legal resolution to the Stewardship.

---

### Question 8 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Aragorn’s description of Éowyn captures:**

**Correct Answer: B. The tragic "frozen" state of her character, where her natural vitality has been petrified by unreturned love and an "ignoble" duty.**

**Why this is correct:**
Aragorn recognizes that her body is being destroyed by the "anguish" of a spirit that has found no outlet for its "High" desires. The "frost" is her own despair at being "trapped."

---

### Question 9 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Merry’s initial refusal to smoke marks a moment of:**

**Correct Answer: B. The weight of personal grief and the "holiness" of memory, where a simple domestic habit is paralyzed by its association with a lost, beloved figure.**

**Why this is correct:**
Merry links his pipe to Théoden's promise to "talk herb-lore." The death of the King has "brought it all back." This is a realistic portrayal of how grief "pollutes" life.

---

### Question 10 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Aragorn’s "stern" joke serves to:**

**Correct Answer: B. Use a familiar, half-humorous "Ranger" persona to ground the hobbit and ease the tension, proving that he remains the "Strider" they knew at Bree.**

**Why this is correct:**
Aragorn’s tone is exactly how he treated them in Book 1. By mocking the Herb-master's pedantry, Aragorn signals to Merry that the world has not changed *so* much that they cannot still "jest" together.
