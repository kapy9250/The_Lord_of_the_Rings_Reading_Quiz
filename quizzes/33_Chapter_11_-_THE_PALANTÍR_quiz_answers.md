### Question 1 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

**Gandalf’s remark that Saruman’s sneer is a "compliment" primarily suggests:**

**Correct Answer: B. That Saruman’s "Great Mind" is fundamentally unsettled by the unpredictable and "unimportant" nature of the hobbits, whose existence challenges his logic of power.**

**Why this is correct:**
Gandalf explains that Saruman is troubled by the "little riddles" of who the hobbits are. Saruman's worldview cannot account for the "rag-tag" hobbits. His "sneer" is a defensive reaction to something he cannot understand or control, making it a backhanded "compliment" to the hobbits' unique role in the world's fate.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Moralistic Misreading" that suspects personal dislike.
- **C** is an "Over-Interpretation" about "recruiting advisors."
- **D** is a "Comedy" distractor about Saruman being "polite."
- **E** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" that misses the intellectual point of the "compliment."

### Question 2 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Pippin’s "quaking knees" reveal:**

**Correct Answer: B. The paralyzing conflict between his moral guilt and the addictive, near-hypnotic "draw" of the Palantír, which has compromised his willpower.**

**Why this is correct:**
Pippin calls himself an "idiotic fool," showing he knows he is doing wrong. Yet, his "knees quaked" and he "did not dare" to put it back. This Physical reaction illustrates the power of the Stone's "draw." He is trapped between his fear of Gandalf and his consuming desire to see into the Stone.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" about "physical exhaustion."
- **C** is a "Lore-Trap" about "White Light."
- **D** is a "Plausible Misreading" about Gandalf "testing his honesty."
- **E** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" about the "weight" of the object.

### Question 3 `[ACN/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**The description of Pippin as a "greedy child stooping over a bowl of food" serves to:**

**Correct Answer: B. Diminish the mythic scale of the artifact by comparing the interaction to a base appetite, emphasizing the "un-Great" but potentially catastrophic nature of his interference.**

**Why this is correct:**
The Palantír is a "Rhyme of Lore" artifact. Comparing Pippin to a "greedy child" highlights the absurdity: a cosmic tool of high wizardry is being "stolen" by a hobbit for a moment of idle curiosity. It underscores the theme that "Great" affairs are often disrupted by mundane impulses.

**Analysis of Distractors:**
- **A** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" about "literal hunger."
- **C** is an "Academic Over-Interpretation" calling the stone a "biological organism."
- **D** is a "Half-Truth" about "sharing the vision" that misses the metaphorical weight.
- **E** is a "Comedy" distractor about "finding food in Mordor."

### Question 4 `[CIM/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**Gandalf’s immediate forgiveness of Pippin indicates that his criteria for judgment are based on:**

Gandalf holds Pippin's gaze and says "There is no lie in your eyes." He recognizes that Pippin acted out of curiosity, not treachery. This differentiates him from Saruman. Gandalf values the "honest" heart, even when it is "foolish," because honesty is the foundation of the fellowship.

### Question 7 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

**Théoden’s observation that "oft evil will shall evil mar" provides a thematic summary of:**

**Correct Answer: B. The inherent self-sabotage of malevolence, where the chaotic impulses of evil agents inadvertently contribute to their own downfall.**

**Why this is correct:**
Gandalf notes that he was saved from his own "grave blunder" by Pippin's "folly." If Gandalf had looked in the Stone, he would have been revealed. Instead, Sauron only saw a captive hobbit. This "strange turn of fortune" is a result of Wormtongue's spite, proving that the internal chaos of evil can produce beneficial results for the "good."
