# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 47_Chapter_4_-_THE_SIEGE_OF_GONDOR.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

Denethor’s description of himself as an "old patient spider" in his grey gloom suggests that his primary mode of leadership is based on:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "There Denethor sat in a grey gloom, like an old patient spider, Pippin thought; he did not seem to have moved since the day before. ... Pippin had an uncomfortable feeling that most of what he had said or done was somehow known..."

- **A.** Active, physical participation in the defense of the walls.
- **B.** A stationary, web-like network of surveillance and long-distance "sight," where he remains at the center of a complex intellectual and spiritual information-gathering system.
- **C.** A desire to physically trap and consume his enemies.
- **D.** Evidence that he is actually related to Shelob.
- **E.** A sign that Denethor is losing his mind and becoming animalistic.

### Question 2 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

Denethor’s insistence that "rustic" songs about food and drink are fit for his halls during "evil times" reveals:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'...We who have lived long under the Shadow may surely listen to echoes from a land untroubled by it? Then we may feel that our vigil was not fruitless, though it may have been thankless.'"

- **A.** His genuine love for hobbit-culture and their "merry" ways.
- **B.** A poignant, perhaps even desperate, need for a reminder of the uncorrupted world that Gondor’s long "vigil" is intended to protect, characterizing the Shire as a symbolic justification for his own suffering.
- **C.** A sign that he is trying to mock Pippin’s simplicity.
- **D.** His belief that the war will be over very soon.
- **E.** Evidence that Denethor is becoming as light-hearted as a hobbit.

### Question 3 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**

- **A.** His disappointment that the armor is not more colorful.

- **C.** A sign that he is actually a very good actor.
- **D.** His belief that Denethor is treating him like a toy.

### Question 4 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

Gandalf’s trembling hands when he learns of Frodo’s path to Cirith Ungol reveal:

### Question 5 `[PIS/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

### Question 6 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Denethor’s decision to send Faramir back to Osgiliath, saying "gentleness may be repaid with death," reveals his:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'...In desperate hours gentleness may be repaid with death.' ... 'I will go and do what I can in his stead - if you command it.' 'I do so,' said Denethor."

- **A.** Genuine belief that Faramir is a better commander than Boromir.
- **B.** A corrosive, "bitter" grief that has turned into a self-destructive cruelty, where he punishes his remaining son for not being the one who died, effectively sacrificing him to prove his own "fell" and un-gentle resolve.
- **C.** His fear that Faramir is actually a coward.
- **D.** A plan to lure the Black Captain into a trap at the river.
- **E.** Evidence that Denethor is losing his memory of the Fords.

### Question 7 `[ACN/SCA]` `[Advanced]`

- **D.** Plan to start a plague inside the City.

### Question 8 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

- **D.** His belief that Faramir is already dead.

### Question 10 `[ACN/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

The sound of the "cock crowing" followed immediately by the "horns of the North" at the end of the chapter symbolizes:

> **Relevant Passage:**

- **A.** A simple change in the weather and the arrival of farm animals.
- **B.** A moment of "Eucatastrophe"—the sudden, miraculous reversal of total despair (the Witch-king’s entry) by the return of the natural order (the dawn) and the fulfillment of ancient alliances (Rohan), marking the beginning of the turning tide.
- **C.** A sign that the battle is actually over.
- **D.** Evidence that the Rohirrim are using birds to signal their arrival.
- **E.** A humorous ending to a very dark and scary scene.
