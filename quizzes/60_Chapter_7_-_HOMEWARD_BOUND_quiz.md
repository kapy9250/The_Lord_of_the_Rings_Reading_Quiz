# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 60_Chapter_7_-_HOMEWARD_BOUND.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Frodo’s reluctance to ride into the Ford of Bruinen and his thought—"There is no real going back... I shall not be the same"—conveys:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "When they came to the Ford of Bruinen, he had halted, and seemed loth to ride into the stream... 'There is no real going back. Though I may come to the Shire, it will not seem the same; for I shall not be the same. ... Where shall I find rest?'"

- **A.** His fear of drowning in the river.
- **B.** A profound ontological "exile," where the spiritual and physical wounding of the quest has permanently separated him from the unfallen world of his origin, making "home" an illusory or unattainable concept.
- **C.** Evidence that Frodo is still suffering from a literal physical illness.
- **D.** His desire to turn back and live in Rivendell forever.
- **E.** A sign that he has forgotten how to ride a horse.

### Question 2 `[ACN/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

The description of the weather turning "wild and wet" as they approach Bree, with a "great curtain of rain" veiling the hill, serves to:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "That night the weather changed... yellow leaves whirled like birds... When they came to the Chetwood already the boughs were almost bare, and a great curtain of rain veiled Bree-hill..."

- **A.** Predict a pleasant stay at *The Prancing Pony*.
- **B.** Tonalize the "shrunken" and dismal reality of the northern lands compared to the glory of the South, using the "bare" and "wet" environment to mirror the decay and "ruffianly" atmosphere the hobbits are about to encounter.
- **C.** Show that the weather in Bree is always bad in October.
- **D.** Suggest that the hobbits are losing their ability to navigate.
- **E.** Provide a humorous contrast to the "fair woodlands" they just left.

### Question 4 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

### Question 6 `[CIM/PIS]` `[Advanced]`

### Question 10 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`
