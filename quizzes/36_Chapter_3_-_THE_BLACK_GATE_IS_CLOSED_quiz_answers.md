### Question 1 `[SCA/ACN]` `[Advanced]`

**The history of the "Teeth of Mordor" symbolizes:**

**Correct Answer: B. The tragic irony of containment, where the very tools of defense built by the "Good" have been co-opted and perfected by the "Evil" to create a state of absolute surveillance.**

**Why this is correct:**
The text notes that the towers were "built by the Men of Gondor" to *prevent* Sauron's return. The fact that they are now used *by* Sauron highlights the theme of the "long defeat" and the ways in which the works of the Wise can be "twisted to evil uses." It emphasizes the tireless vigilance of the Shadow.

- **A** is a "Surface-Level Literalism" about "durable engineering."
- **C** is a "Lore-Trap" about "Orcish architectural knowledge."
- **D** is an "Over-Interpretation" about Men "initiating the war."
- **E** is a "Comedy" distractor about "real-estate ownership."

### Question 2 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

**Sam’s thoughts of his Gaffer primarily reveal:**

**Correct Answer: B. A psychological grounding technique where the familiar, domestic "nagging" of home acts as a protective buffer against the overwhelming despair of the Morannon.**

In the face of "the bitter end," Sam turns to the memory of the Gaffer. By imagining his father's predictable reaction, Sam reduces the cosmic horror of Mordor to a scale he can manage. This "Shire-mindedness" is Sam's primary defense; he maintains his identity by tethering his thoughts to mundane details.
