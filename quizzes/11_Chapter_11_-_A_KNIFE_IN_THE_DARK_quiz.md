# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 11_Chapter_11_-_A_KNIFE_IN_THE_DARK.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

### Question 1 `[ACN/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "Outside the gate they stopped, and three black figures entered, like shades of night creeping across the ground. ... 'Open, in the name of Mordor!' said a voice thin and menacing."

- **A.** Show that the Black Riders are easily deterred by locked doors.
- **B.** Contrast the Shire’s internal "Horn-call" with the external, absolute authority of "Mordor," highlighting the vulnerability of the hobbits' civilization.
- **C.** Explain why Fatty Bolger was the wrong choice to stay behind.
- **D.** Suggest that the Black Riders are actually afraid of the Brandybucks.
- **E.** Provide a humorous example of a botched home invasion.
