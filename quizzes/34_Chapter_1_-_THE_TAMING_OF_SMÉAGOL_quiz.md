# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 34_Chapter_1_-_THE_TAMING_OF_SMÉAGOL.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

Frodo’s "shudder" when he looks toward Mordor and mutters "If I must go there, I wish I could come there quickly and make an end!" reveals:

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'Mordor!' he muttered under his breath. 'If I must go there, I wish I could come there quickly and make an end!' He shuddered. The wind was chilly and yet heavy with an odour of cold decay."

- **A.** A renewed sense of heroism and a desire for a glorious battle.
- **B.** A profound psychological weariness and a longing for the finality of the quest, even if that end is death, suggesting that the "delay" is more painful than the destination itself.
- **C.** His anger at Sam for bringing up the cooking-gear.
- **D.** A belief that he can actually defeat Sauron quickly.
- **E.** A sign that he is catching a cold from the eastern wind.
