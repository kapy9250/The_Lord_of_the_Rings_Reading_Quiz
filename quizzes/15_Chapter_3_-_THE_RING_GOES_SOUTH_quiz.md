# Reading Comprehension Quiz: 15_Chapter_3_-_THE_RING_GOES_SOUTH.md

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Questions:** 10 | **Time suggested:** 30–40 minutes

**Instructions:** Each question below is followed by a relevant passage from the text. Choose the single best answer (A–E) for each question. Focus on what the text *implies* rather than what it explicitly states. Consider tone, subtext, character psychology, and authorial choices in your reasoning.

---

### Question 1 `[CIM/ANT]` `[Advanced]`

> **Relevant Passage:**
> "'It's most unfair,' said Pippin. 'Instead of throwing him out... Elrond goes and _rewards_ him for his cheek!' 'Rewards!' said Frodo. 'I can't imagine a more severe punishment. ...' 'I don't wonder,' said Merry... 'But we are envying Sam, not you.'"

- **A.** Pippin’s fundamental lack of understanding regarding the danger of the quest.
- **B.** The powerful bond of kinship and the idea that social exclusion from one's friends is a more painful "punishment" than shared physical hardship.
- **C.** A competitive desire to prove that he is more "intelligent" than Sam.
- **D.** His hope that Rivendell will eventually become a boring place to live.
- **E.** A sign that Pippin is actually more interested in the Ring than Sam is.
